Imitate

For this experiment, I’m basing on my inspiration from the two teapot examples in lecture. I was thinking about making 3D objects doing a circle formation, so the examples above can assist me in learning about how to apply multiple 3D objects and how they would rotate on screen.

Integrate

From the teapot in the twister example, I had the code to only rotate the objects around the z-axis and it gave me the circle formation I was looking for. I wonder how I can have the rotation speed be faster than it is now.After finding out that changing the value within rotateZ() will increase the speed of rotation, I implemented the teapot class with its property to the teapot in the sky example. This helps me visualize more of the algorithm, and wondering how I can place each pot at a specific location on the canvas.When approaching the idea of placing multiple teapots at specific locations of the canvas, the teapot in the twister example gives me the method of assigning each pot as a value in a list where I can go through each to put them on the canvas. The positions were found out by testing what vectors will place each pot, so it might change when I choose my own model.
